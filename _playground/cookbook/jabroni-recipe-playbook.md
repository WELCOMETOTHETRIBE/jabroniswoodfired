## 01 — PIZZA DOUGH
### The Foundation

*Every pizza on this menu starts here. 48-hour cold ferment. Hand-stretched to order. No rolling pin — ever.*

---

**Yield:** 6 dough balls (280g each) — makes 6 individual 12" pies

**INGREDIENTS**

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Tipo "00" flour (Caputo Pizzeria or King Arthur) | 1,000g |
| Cold filtered water | 650g (65% hydration) |
| Fine sea salt | 28g |
| Active dry yeast | 3g |
| Extra virgin olive oil | 15g |

**METHOD**

1. Dissolve yeast in 50g of the cold water. Let bloom 5 minutes.
2. In a large bowl, combine flour and salt. Make a well in the center.
3. Pour in yeast mixture, remaining water, and olive oil. Mix until shaggy.
4. Turn dough onto a clean surface. Knead 10–12 minutes until smooth, elastic, and passes the windowpane test.
5. Divide into 280g balls. Coat lightly in olive oil. Place in individual lidded containers.
6. **Cold ferment in the refrigerator for 48–72 hours.** This is non-negotiable.
7. Pull dough 2 hours before service. Temper at room temperature before stretching.

**STRETCH**
- Press flat with fingers from center outward. Never touch the cornicione (outer crust edge).
- Drape over knuckles. Rotate. Let gravity do the work.
- Target: 12 inches, thin center, puffed edge.

---

**Fire note:** Wood-fired oven at 850–900°F. Pizza bakes in 60–90 seconds. Watch the cornicione — it should char in spots. That's the point.

## 02 — FOCACCIA
### Italian Tradition. Wood-Fired Artistry.

*Crisp bottom. Pillowy interior. Dimpled and drowned in olive oil. The first thing that hits the table.*

---

**Yield:** 2 half-sheet pans

**INGREDIENTS**

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Tipo "00" flour | 500g |
| Warm water | 400g (80% hydration) |
| Fine sea salt | 14g |
| Active dry yeast | 5g |
| Extra virgin olive oil | 60g (plus more for the pan) |
| Flaky sea salt (Maldon) | for finishing |
| Fresh rosemary, picked | 2 tbsp |
| Garlic cloves, thin-sliced | 4 cloves |

**METHOD**

1. Mix yeast with warm water. Bloom 5 minutes.
2. Add flour, salt, and 30g of the olive oil. Mix to combine — this dough is wet and sticky. Do not over-knead.
3. Perform 4 sets of stretch-and-fold every 30 minutes over 2 hours at room temperature.
4. Generously coat a half-sheet pan with olive oil. Pour dough in. Stretch to fill pan loosely.
5. Cover with plastic wrap. Rest 30 minutes.
6. Dimple aggressively with oiled fingers — press all the way to the pan. Drizzle remaining olive oil into every dimple.
7. Top with sliced garlic and fresh rosemary.
8. **Cold proof overnight** or bake immediately for a denser crumb.
9. Bake in wood-fired oven at 550°F (lower dome zone) for 15–18 minutes, rotating once. Bottom should be deep golden. Top should blister.

**Finish:** Flaky sea salt the moment it comes out of the oven. Slice and serve hot.

## 03 — FIRE-ROASTED VEGGIES
### Flame-Charred. Smoky. Bold.

*Whatever is in season. Whatever has never tasted like this before.*

---

**Yield:** Serves 20–25 as a side

**INGREDIENTS**

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Zucchini, halved lengthwise | 4 large |
| Japanese eggplant, halved | 4 medium |
| Red & yellow bell peppers, halved & seeded | 6 total |
| Fennel bulb, thick-sliced | 2 bulbs |
| Broccolini or asparagus | 2 bunches |
| Red onion, thick-sliced | 3 medium |
| Extra virgin olive oil | 1/3 cup |
| Kosher salt | 2 tsp |
| Cracked black pepper | 1 tsp |
| Fresh thyme sprigs | 8–10 |
| Aged balsamic vinegar | 3 tbsp (finish) |
| Flaky sea salt | for finish |
| Parmigiano Reggiano, shaved | for finish |

**METHOD**

1. Toss all vegetables in olive oil, kosher salt, pepper, and thyme leaves.
2. Lay directly on the cast-iron grate or a heavy sheet pan in the wood-fired oven at 600°F.
3. Let them sit without touching for 4–5 minutes. You want char — not just color.
4. Turn once. Roast another 3–4 minutes until tender and blistered.
5. Pull from fire. Arrange on a platter in overlapping layers.
6. Drizzle aged balsamic. Finish with flaky salt and shaved Parmigiano.

**Optional additions:** Cherry tomatoes, whole heads of garlic (roasted and squeezed), lemon zest.

## 04 — PITMASTER'S PRIDE
### 12-Hour Smoked Brisket with Burnt Ends

*Hickory smoke. House rub. The whole packer. This is the centerpiece.*

---

**Yield:** One whole packer brisket, 14–18 lbs (serves 30–40)

**HOUSE RUB**

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Coarse black pepper | 1/2 cup |
| Kosher salt | 1/4 cup |
| Garlic powder | 2 tbsp |
| Onion powder | 2 tbsp |
| Smoked paprika | 2 tbsp |
| Cayenne pepper | 1 tsp |
| Brown sugar | 1 tbsp |

**BRISKET METHOD**

1. The day before service, trim brisket fat cap to 1/4 inch. Leave the point fully intact.
2. Apply house rub generously on all surfaces. Pat, don't rub — the moisture does the work.
3. Wrap in butcher paper and refrigerate overnight uncovered (dry brine).
4. Pull brisket 1 hour before smoke.
5. Load offset smoker or wood-fired chamber with **hickory** at 225–250°F.
6. Place brisket fat-side up. Do not touch for 6 hours.
7. At internal temp of 165°F (the stall), wrap tightly in butcher paper (not foil — paper breathes).
8. Continue smoking until internal temp hits **203°F**, approximately 10–14 hours total.
9. Rest wrapped in a cooler for a **minimum of 2 hours**. This is not optional.
10. Slice flat against the grain. Thick as a pencil.

**BURNT ENDS**

1. Separate the point from the flat after the full cook.
2. Cube the point into 1.5-inch pieces.
3. Toss in a mix of: your favorite BBQ sauce, brown sugar, a pad of butter.
4. Return to smoker or cast-iron pan over direct fire at 300°F for 45–60 minutes, uncovered.
5. They're done when caramelized, lacquered, and dangerous.

---

**Wood:** Hickory. No compromise. Post-oak acceptable. Never mesquite for brisket.

## 05 — HOG'S SHARE
### Smoked Pulled Pork & Flame-Seared Pork Belly

*Two cuts. One pig. Smoke on the shoulder. Fire on the belly.*

---

**Yield:** One bone-in pork shoulder (8–10 lbs) + one pork belly slab (5–6 lbs)

---

### PULLED PORK

**PORK RUB**

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Brown sugar | 3 tbsp |
| Kosher salt | 2 tbsp |
| Smoked paprika | 2 tbsp |
| Black pepper | 1 tbsp |
| Garlic powder | 1 tbsp |
| Onion powder | 1 tbsp |
| Dry mustard | 1 tsp |
| Cayenne | 1 tsp |
| Cumin | 1 tsp |

**METHOD**

1. Score fat cap with a crosshatch pattern. Apply rub all over. Wrap and refrigerate overnight.
2. Smoke bone-in shoulder at 225°F over hickory for 8–10 hours.
3. Wrap in butcher paper at the stall (around 165°F internal). Continue to 205°F.
4. Rest 1 hour. Pull by hand — the bone should slide clean out.
5. Season pulled pork with a splash of apple cider vinegar and a ladle of pan drippings.

---

### SMOKED & SEARED PORK BELLY

**INGREDIENTS**

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Skinless pork belly slab | 5–6 lbs |
| Kosher salt | 2 tbsp |
| Black pepper | 1 tbsp |
| Brown sugar | 2 tbsp |
| Garlic powder | 1 tsp |
| Smoked paprika | 1 tsp |

**METHOD**

1. Score belly in a crosshatch 1/2 inch deep. Season liberally with dry rub.
2. Smoke at 250°F for 3 hours.
3. Remove from smoker. Let cool slightly. Slice into service portions.
4. Sear over live oak coals or in a screaming-hot cast-iron pan — 3–4 minutes per side.
5. The outside should be deeply caramelized and slightly charred. The inside should pull apart.

**Serve:** Pulled pork and pork belly side by side. Let guests mix and compare.

## 06 — FEATHER & FLAME
### Fire-Roasted Chicken & Smoked Turkey

*Brined, seasoned, and kissed by fire. No dry bird leaves this operation.*

---

**Yield:** 4 whole chickens + 2 turkey breasts

---

### FIRE-ROASTED CHICKEN

**BRINE (per chicken)**

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Water | 1 gallon |
| Kosher salt | 1/2 cup |
| Brown sugar | 1/4 cup |
| Black peppercorns | 1 tbsp |
| Fresh thyme | 4 sprigs |
| Bay leaves | 3 |
| Garlic, smashed | 6 cloves |
| Lemon, halved | 1 |

**METHOD**

1. Spatchcock chickens: remove backbone with kitchen shears. Press flat.
2. Submerge in brine. Refrigerate 6–12 hours.
3. Pat completely dry. Season: salt, black pepper, smoked paprika, garlic powder, dried oregano.
4. Roast in wood-fired oven at 500–550°F for 35–45 minutes, rotating halfway.
5. Pull at 165°F internal in the thigh.
6. Rest 10 minutes before breaking down.

---

### SMOKED TURKEY BREAST

**METHOD**

1. Brine turkey breast using same recipe above, scaled up, for 12–24 hours.
2. Dry thoroughly. Rub with butter, fresh thyme, garlic, salt, and pepper under and over the skin.
3. Smoke at 250°F over hickory for 3–4 hours until internal temp reads 165°F.
4. Rest 20 minutes. Slice thick, across the grain.

**Service note:** A drizzle of pan drippings or turkey jus over the sliced breast goes a long way.

## 07 — CALABRIAN SLAW
### Crisp. Heat. Acid. Balance.

*Not your uncle's coleslaw. Pecorino, Calabrian chili, fennel, and honey.*

---

**Yield:** Serves 30–35 as a side

**INGREDIENTS**

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Green cabbage, thinly shaved | 1 large head |
| Purple cabbage, thinly shaved | 1/2 head |
| Fennel bulb, thinly shaved (fronds reserved) | 2 bulbs |
| Carrots, julienned | 4 large |
| Fresh basil, chiffonade | 1 cup |
| Pecorino Romano, finely grated | 1/2 cup |

**CALABRIAN CHILI-LEMON DRESSING**

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Calabrian chili paste | 2 tbsp |
| Fresh lemon juice | 1/3 cup |
| Apple cider vinegar | 3 tbsp |
| Wildflower honey | 2 tbsp |
| Extra virgin olive oil | 1/2 cup |
| Dijon mustard | 1 tsp |
| Kosher salt | 1 tsp |
| Cracked black pepper | 1/2 tsp |

**METHOD**

1. Combine cabbages, fennel, and carrots in a large bowl.
2. Whisk dressing ingredients until emulsified.
3. Toss slaw with just enough dressing to coat — not drown.
4. Let sit 15–20 minutes before service to wilt slightly.
5. Toss in basil and grated Pecorino right before plating.
6. Drizzle additional honey over the top. Finish with fennel fronds.

**Make ahead:** Slaw base holds up to 4 hours dressed. Add herbs at the last minute.

## 08 — BELLY BEANS
### Slow-Cooked Baked Beans with Smoked Pork Belly

*Humble beans, elevated by fire, pork belly, balsamic, and time.*

---

**Yield:** Serves 30–35 as a side

**INGREDIENTS**

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Dried navy beans or great northern beans | 2 lbs (soaked overnight) |
| Smoked pork belly (from Hog's Share), cubed | 1 lb |
| Yellow onion, fine dice | 2 large |
| Red bell pepper, fire-roasted, peeled & diced | 3 peppers |
| Garlic cloves, minced | 8 cloves |
| Smoky BBQ sauce | 1 cup |
| Balsamic vinegar (good quality) | 3 tbsp |
| Brown sugar | 3 tbsp |
| Dijon mustard | 2 tbsp |
| Smoked paprika | 1 tbsp |
| Kosher salt & black pepper | to taste |
| Chicken or pork stock | 4 cups |
| Crushed San Marzano tomatoes | 1 can (28 oz) |

**METHOD**

1. Drain soaked beans. Cover with fresh water, bring to boil, simmer 45 minutes until just tender. Drain.
2. In a large cast-iron Dutch oven, render pork belly cubes until fat releases and edges crisp.
3. Add onion and garlic. Sweat 8 minutes over medium heat.
4. Add fire-roasted peppers, BBQ sauce, crushed tomatoes, stock, balsamic, brown sugar, mustard, and paprika.
5. Fold in par-cooked beans. Bring to a simmer.
6. Transfer pot to the edge of the wood-fired oven (400°F zone) or low oven. Cook uncovered for 1.5–2 hours, stirring occasionally.
7. The liquid should reduce to a thick, glossy gravy that coats every bean.
8. Taste. Adjust salt, pepper, and balsamic. The beans should be smoky, slightly sweet, slightly acidic.

**Finish:** Scatter a few additional cubes of crispy pork belly on top before service.

## 09 — SMOKE & CACIO
### Mac n' Cheese — Reimagined

*Al dente pasta. Smoked cheeses. Béchamel. Sharp Pecorino. This is serious mac.*

---

**Yield:** Serves 30–35 as a side

**INGREDIENTS**

**Pasta**

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Cavatappi or rigatoni | 3 lbs dried |
| Kosher salt | for pasta water |

**Smoked Béchamel**

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Unsalted butter | 8 tbsp |
| All-purpose flour | 8 tbsp |
| Whole milk, warm | 6 cups |
| Heavy cream | 2 cups |
| Smoked gouda, shredded | 1 lb |
| Sharp smoked cheddar, shredded | 1/2 lb |
| Pecorino Romano, finely grated | 3/4 cup |
| Dijon mustard | 1 tbsp |
| Smoked paprika | 1 tsp |
| Nutmeg, freshly grated | 1/4 tsp |
| Kosher salt & white pepper | to taste |

**Topping**

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Panko breadcrumbs | 1 cup |
| Pecorino Romano, grated | 1/4 cup |
| Unsalted butter, melted | 3 tbsp |
| Fresh thyme leaves | 1 tsp |

**METHOD**

1. Cook pasta in heavily salted water until 2 minutes shy of al dente. Drain, reserving 1 cup pasta water.
2. **Béchamel:** Melt butter in a large heavy pot over medium heat. Whisk in flour. Cook roux 2 minutes — it should smell nutty.
3. Slowly whisk in warm milk and cream, 1 cup at a time, until smooth and thick. Cook 8–10 minutes, stirring constantly.
4. Off heat, fold in smoked gouda, smoked cheddar, and Pecorino. Stir until completely melted.
5. Add mustard, paprika, nutmeg. Season aggressively with salt and white pepper.
6. Fold pasta into cheese sauce. Add a splash of pasta water if needed to loosen.
7. Transfer to large baking dishes or a cast-iron pan.
8. Combine topping ingredients. Scatter evenly over mac.
9. Bake in wood-fired oven or 425°F conventional oven for 20–25 minutes until bubbling and top is deep golden.
10. Rest 5 minutes before service.

**Optional:** Finish with a drizzle of hot honey and extra Pecorino at the table.

# PART III — WOOD-FIRED PIZZAS

> **Standard build for all pizzas:**
> 1. Stretch dough to 12 inches.
> 2. Apply sauce or base to within 1/2 inch of edge.
> 3. Add toppings — less is more. Don't overload.
> 4. Bake in wood-fired oven at 850–900°F for 60–90 seconds, rotating every 20 seconds.
> 5. Finish with any post-bake toppings immediately after pulling.
> 6. Slice and serve within 90 seconds.

## 10 — MARINARA
### No Cheese. Just Fire.

*The oldest pizza on earth. No cheese. No compromise. Pure tomato, garlic, and fire.*

---

**SAUCE**

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| San Marzano DOP tomatoes (whole, canned) | 1 can (28 oz) |
| Garlic cloves, thin-sliced | 5 cloves |
| Dried Sicilian oregano (on the branch if possible) | 1 tbsp |
| Extra virgin olive oil | 3 tbsp |
| Kosher salt | 1 tsp |

**METHOD**

1. Crush tomatoes by hand. No blender — you want texture and some chunks.
2. Stir in salt and half the olive oil. Taste. Adjust salt.
3. Spread a thin layer on stretched dough.
4. Scatter garlic slivers evenly across the sauce.
5. Crumble oregano over the top.
6. Drizzle remaining olive oil.
7. Fire immediately.

**Post-bake:** Nothing. This pizza is done when it comes out of the oven.

*Notes for Patrick:* The Marinara is the litmus test for the whole operation. If the tomatoes are right and the fire is right, this pizza needs nothing else.

## 11 — MARGHERITA
### The Standard. The Benchmark.

*San Marzano. Fior di latte. Basil. Olive oil. The whole story in four ingredients.*

---

**INGREDIENTS**

| Ingredient | Amount (per pie) |
|---|---|
| San Marzano tomato sauce | 4 tbsp |
| Fresh fior di latte mozzarella, torn | 4 oz |
| Fresh basil leaves | 6–8 leaves |
| Extra virgin olive oil | 1 tbsp |
| Flaky sea salt | pinch |

**SAUCE:** Crush San Marzano tomatoes by hand. Season with salt and a touch of olive oil. That's it.

**METHOD**

1. Stretch dough.
2. Spread sauce thin — you should almost see through it.
3. Tear mozzarella into irregular pieces. Distribute. Leave gaps — the tomato should show.
4. Fire at 850–900°F for 60–90 seconds.
5. **Post-bake:** Lay fresh basil leaves directly on the hot pie. Drizzle olive oil. Pinch of flaky salt.

**The rule:** If you slice before the basil wilts, you went too fast.

## 12 — HOT 'RONI
### Crispy. Charred. Spicy. Honey.

*Pepperoni cups. Char bubbles. Spicy honey drizzle. The crowd-pleaser that earns its place.*

---

**INGREDIENTS**

| Ingredient | Amount (per pie) |
|---|---|
| San Marzano tomato sauce | 4 tbsp |
| Low-moisture whole-milk mozzarella, shredded | 5 oz |
| Cup-and-char pepperoni (Ezzo or similar) | 20–25 slices |
| **Spicy Honey Drizzle** | |
| Wildflower honey | 2 tbsp |
| Red pepper flakes (or Calabrian chili) | 1/2 tsp |
| Hot sauce | dash |

**METHOD**

1. Stretch dough. Sauce thin.
2. Layer mozzarella evenly.
3. Place pepperoni on top — overlapping slightly. They will cup and fill with rendered fat in the oven.
4. Fire at 850–900°F. Those pepperoni cups should hold little pools of grease and char at the edges.
5. **Spicy honey:** Warm honey gently with red pepper flakes and hot sauce. Stir to combine.
6. **Post-bake:** Drizzle spicy honey immediately over the hot pie. Serve.

## 13 — HOLY SMOKE
### Smoked Brisket. Caramelized Onions. Mustard Crema.

*The crossover. BBQ on a pizza. Done with the seriousness it deserves.*

---

**INGREDIENTS**

| Ingredient | Amount (per pie) |
|---|---|
| Smoked brisket (from Pitmaster's Pride), thin-sliced or hand-pulled | 4 oz |
| Caramelized onions | 3 tbsp |
| Creamy low-moisture mozzarella, shredded | 4 oz |
| Signature mustard crema | 4 tbsp (base) |

**CARAMELIZED ONIONS**

1. Slice 6 yellow onions thin. Cook in 3 tbsp butter + 1 tbsp olive oil over low heat.
2. Stir every 5 minutes. Patience. This takes 45–60 minutes.
3. They should be deep amber, jammy, and sweet. Season with salt and a splash of balsamic.

**SIGNATURE MUSTARD CREMA**

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Whole-grain Dijon mustard | 2 tbsp |
| Yellow mustard | 1 tbsp |
| Heavy cream | 3 tbsp |
| Honey | 1 tsp |
| Garlic (fine grated, 1 clove) | 1 clove |
| Salt | to taste |

Whisk all together. Taste. It should be tangy, creamy, and slightly sweet.

**METHOD**

1. Stretch dough. Spread mustard crema as the base.
2. Layer mozzarella.
3. Add caramelized onions in small clumps.
4. Place sliced brisket on top — don't over-crowd.
5. Fire 60–90 seconds at 850–900°F.
6. **Post-bake:** A thin additional drizzle of mustard crema and fresh thyme.

## 14 — THE OUTLAW
### Jalapeño Cheddar Sausage. Charred Peppers. Smoked Mozzarella.

*Texas sausage on an Italian pizza. Neither culture asked for this. Both will thank you.*

---

**INGREDIENTS**

| Ingredient | Amount (per pie) |
|---|---|
| San Marzano tomato sauce | 4 tbsp |
| Smoked mozzarella, shredded | 4 oz |
| Jalapeño cheddar sausage (bulk, removed from casing) | 4 oz |
| Fire-roasted red peppers, torn | 3 strips |

**FIRE-ROASTING PEPPERS**
Place whole red peppers directly on the wood-fired oven grate. Char on all sides, 8–10 minutes total. Seal in a bag 15 minutes. Peel, seed, tear into strips.

**METHOD**

1. Stretch dough. Sauce thin.
2. Layer smoked mozzarella.
3. Pinch small pieces of raw jalapeño cheddar sausage across the pie — it will cook and render in the fire.
4. Lay torn fire-roasted pepper strips across the pie.
5. Fire 60–90 seconds at 850–900°F.
6. **Post-bake:** Nothing needed. This pie finishes itself.

*Optional finish:* A handful of sliced fresh jalapeños for those who want more heat.

## 16 — NONNA'S RANSOM
### Prosciutto. Arugula. Fig & Onion Base. Balsamic.

*No tomato. No meat on the fire. This pizza earns its elegance in restraint.*

---

**INGREDIENTS**

| Ingredient | Amount (per pie) |
|---|---|
| Caramelized onion & fig base | 4 tbsp |
| Fresh mozzarella (fior di latte), torn | 3 oz |
| Prosciutto di Parma, thin-sliced | 4–5 slices |
| Fresh arugula | small handful |
| Aged balsamic vinegar | drizzle |
| Extra virgin olive oil | drizzle |
| Parmigiano Reggiano, shaved | garnish |

**CARAMELIZED ONION & FIG BASE**

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Caramelized onions (see Holy Smoke recipe) | 1 cup |
| Black mission fig jam or fresh fig, smashed | 3 tbsp |
| Fresh thyme | 1 tsp |
| Salt | to taste |

8. Serve in under 60 seconds. The arugula should be barely wilted at the edges.

## 17 — CAPO DI FUEGO
### Eggplant. Smoked Mozzarella. Roasted Red Peppers. Pesto. Aged Balsamic.

*The vegetarian that never apologizes for what it is.*

---

**INGREDIENTS**

| Smoked mozzarella, shredded | 4 oz |
| Fire-roasted eggplant | 4–5 pieces |

**BASIL PESTO BASE**

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Fresh basil (packed) | 2 cups |
| Pine nuts, toasted | 1/4 cup |
| Garlic cloves | 2 |
| Parmigiano Reggiano, grated | 1/2 cup |

| Lemon juice | 1 tbsp |
| Salt | to taste |

Blend smooth. Taste. The pesto should be bright, nutty, slightly sharp.

**FIRE-ROASTED EGGPLANT**
Slice eggplant into 1/2-inch rounds. Brush with olive oil, salt, pepper. Place on a wire rack in the wood-fired oven at 600°F. Roast 8–10 minutes, flipping once. Pull when deeply caramelized and collapsed.

**METHOD**

1. Stretch dough. Spread pesto as the base.
2. Layer smoked mozzarella.
3. Place eggplant rounds and roasted pepper strips across the pie.
4. Fire 60–90 seconds at 850–900°F.
5. **Post-bake:** Drizzle aged balsamic. Lay fresh basil leaves. Serve.

## 18 — PRINCESS PEACH
### Puff Pastry. Cinnamon Sugar. Wood-Fired Poached Peaches. Mascarpone Crema.

*Flaky. Sweet. Fire-kissed fruit. The one that makes people ask "wait, where did this come from?"*

---

**Yield:** 12–15 individual pastries

**INGREDIENTS**

**Puff Pastry Base**

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Frozen all-butter puff pastry, thawed | 2 sheets (14 oz each) |
| Egg, beaten (egg wash) | 1 |
| Unsalted butter, melted | 2 tbsp |
| Cinnamon sugar (2 tsp cinnamon : 4 tbsp sugar) | 1/3 cup |

**Wood-Fired Poached Peaches**

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Fresh ripe peaches, halved & pitted | 8 peaches |
| Dry white wine | 1 cup |
| Wildflower honey | 3 tbsp |
| Vanilla bean, split | 1 |
| Cinnamon stick | 1 |
| Lemon zest | 1 lemon |
| Pinch of salt | — |

**Mascarpone Crema**

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Mascarpone | 8 oz |
| Heavy cream | 3 tbsp |
| Powdered sugar | 3 tbsp |
| Vanilla extract | 1 tsp |
| Lemon zest | 1/2 lemon |

**METHOD**

**Poached Peaches:**
1. Combine wine, honey, vanilla, cinnamon, lemon zest, and salt in a cast-iron skillet.
2. Place peach halves cut-side down in the liquid.
3. Slide into a medium-heat zone of the wood-fired oven (400°F). Cook 20–25 minutes, basting twice.
4. Peaches should be tender and caramelized on the cut face. Liquid should reduce to a glaze.
5. Cool slightly before slicing.

**Mascarpone Crema:**
Whisk mascarpone until smooth. Add cream, powdered sugar, vanilla, and lemon zest. Whisk until fluffy and spreadable. Refrigerate until service.

**Pastry:**
1. Cut puff pastry into rectangles or squares. Score a border 1/2 inch in.
2. Brush with egg wash. Scatter cinnamon sugar generously inside the border.
3. Bake in 400°F oven (or cooler zone of wood-fired oven) until deeply golden and puffed, 18–22 minutes.
4. Cool 5 minutes. Press the center down gently.

**Assemble:**
Spoon mascarpone crema onto pastry. Layer sliced poached peaches over cream. Drizzle reduced poaching liquid over the top. Serve warm or at room temperature.

## 19 — KENNY'S CHEESECAKE
### Rich. Velvety. Fire-Roasted Fruit Compote.

*A New York cheesecake that earns a place at a wood-fired table. The compote is the hook.*

---

**Yield:** Two 9-inch cheesecakes (serves 30–35 slices)

**INGREDIENTS**

**Crust**

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Graham crackers, crushed fine | 2 cups |
| Unsalted butter, melted | 6 tbsp |
| Sugar | 2 tbsp |
| Pinch of salt | — |

**Filling (per cheesecake)**

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Cream cheese, room temperature | 32 oz (4 blocks) |
| Granulated sugar | 1 1/4 cups |
| Sour cream | 1/2 cup |
| Large eggs | 4 |
| Vanilla extract | 2 tsp |
| Lemon zest | 1 lemon |
| Lemon juice | 1 tbsp |
| All-purpose flour | 2 tbsp |
| Pinch of salt | — |

**Fire-Roasted Fruit Compote**

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Stone fruit (peaches, plums, cherries) or berries | 2 lbs |
| Granulated sugar | 1/4 cup |
| Honey | 2 tbsp |
| Balsamic vinegar | 1 tbsp |
| Lemon juice | 1 tbsp |

**METHOD**

**Crust:**
Mix graham crumbs, butter, sugar, and salt. Press into springform pans. Bake 10 minutes at 325°F. Cool.

**Filling:**
1. Beat cream cheese until completely smooth (no lumps).
2. Add sugar. Beat 2 minutes.
3. Add sour cream, flour, vanilla, lemon zest and juice. Mix.
4. Add eggs one at a time, mixing slowly after each. Do not overbeat.
5. Pour over prepared crust.
6. Bake in a water bath at 325°F for 65–75 minutes — edges should be set, center should have a 3-inch wobble.
7. Turn oven off. Leave cheesecake inside with door cracked for 1 hour.
8. Refrigerate minimum 4 hours, preferably overnight.

**Fire-Roasted Fruit Compote:**
1. Halve stone fruit or leave berries whole.
2. Toss with sugar, honey, and a pinch of salt.
3. Lay on a cast-iron pan. Roast directly in wood-fired oven at 500–550°F for 10–15 minutes.
4. Fruit should be blistered, juicy, and slightly charred at the edges.
5. Transfer to a saucepan. Add balsamic and lemon juice. Simmer 5 minutes until syrupy.
6. Cool slightly before spooning over cheesecake at service.

**Service:** Slice cold. Spoon warm compote over each slice tableside.

## 20 — CAMPFIRE CARUSO
### Pizza Dough. Dark Chocolate. Toasted Marshmallow. Graham Cracker. Fire-Kissed.

*S'mores, if s'mores went to culinary school and found religion.*

---

**Yield:** 12–15 individual pieces

**INGREDIENTS**

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Pizza dough balls (200g each — smaller than pizza) | 12–15 |
| Dark chocolate (70%+), broken into shards | 1 lb |
| Large marshmallows | 2–3 per piece |
| Graham crackers, crushed | 1 cup |
| Unsalted butter, melted | 2 tbsp |
| Egg, beaten (egg wash) | 1 |
| Flaky sea salt | for finish |
| Powdered sugar | for dusting |

**METHOD**

1. Stretch each dough ball into a small oval or circle, about 8 inches.
2. On one half: place 4–5 chocolate shards, 2–3 marshmallows, and a tablespoon of crushed graham crackers.
3. Fold dough over the filling. Press and crimp edges to seal. No gaps — the chocolate will escape.
4. Brush sealed edge and top with egg wash.
5. Dust lightly with crushed graham crackers on top.
6. Bake in wood-fired oven at 700°F for 3–4 minutes, watching closely. The dough should be blistered and golden; the marshmallows should be melted and starting to push against the seams.
7. Pull from oven. Let rest 2 minutes — the inside is lava.
8. **Finish:** Flaky sea salt over the top. Dust of powdered sugar.

**Service note:** Cut open tableside if possible. The marshmallow and chocolate pour out. That's the moment.
